Bulletin 2

Is this one reason Cholsey had flooded more over the past few years?

Industrial Fly Tipping in Cholsey, South Oxfordshire
A million litres of rain water can’t escape down Cholsey Brook as fast as it used to, into the Thames flood plain, because of 1000’s of tons of illegal industrial fly tipping that took place in the flood plain between May 2020 and May 2021.

This is a clip from Google Earth clearly showing the infilling in progress, of 0.7m deep waste material. Put OX10 9HG into the Google Earth search tab and select years around 2020-2022.
  • The Environment Agency (EA) were immediately notified and a FOI Request asked if any permission to infill the flood plain had been granted.
    • No permission was granted, so the land owners and the company that supplied the waste have infilled the flood plain illegally.
  • The EA confirmed they visited the site and were told by the owners that only drainage ditches were dredged, while neighbours reported hundreds of lorries over 6 months.
    • Since then, the EA has done nothing.
  • Cholsey Parish Council reported this to Oxfordshire County Council who have done nothing.
  • The OCC were sent a FOI request at the end of 2025 but refuse to say what action they are taking.
  • It appears both the land owners and the company supplying the waste (in unmarked lorries) have committed a crime.
  • Had this infilling been done legally, it would have incurred approx. £1/4 million in landfill tax.
  • This video shows the 2 adjacent properties where the Thames Flood Plain has been illegally infilled 0.7m high in one to stop flooding of the property.
Conclusion
  • The appropriate staff at Oxfordshire County Council responsible for prosecuting such blatant criminal activity must explain to the people of Oxfordshire what they are doing.
  • Residents of the Thames Valley, especially Cholsey, may wish to e-mail commentsandcomplaints@oxfordshire.gov.uk to ask the same question.
  • The owners that infilled illegally may have problems selling their property if there is a £1/4 million tax bill or potential prosecution.
  • If no action is taken soon, those responsible, including the owners, the EA, and the Council, should be named.
