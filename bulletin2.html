Bulletin 2

Is this one reason Cholsey had flooded more over the past few years?

Industrial Fly Tipping in Cholsey, South Oxfordshire
A million litres of rain water can’t escape down Cholsey Brook as fast as it used to, into the Thames flood plain, because of 1000’s of tons of illegal industrial fly tipping that took place in the flood plain between May 2020 and May 2021.

This is a clip from Google Earth clearly showing the infilling in progress, of 0.7m deep waste material. Put OX10 9HG into the Google Earth search tab and select years around 2020-2022.
  • The Environment Agency (EA) were immediately notified and a FOI Request asked if any permission had been granted.
    • No permission was granted; landowners and company infilled illegally.
  • The EA visited but did nothing after reports.
  • Cholsey Parish Council reported to OCC, no action.
  • OCC refused to disclose actions via FOI.
  • Infilling would have cost ~£1/4 million landfill tax legally.
  • Video shows properties with illegal infill.
Conclusion
  • The appropriate staff at OCC must explain actions.
  • Residents may e-mail commentsandcomplaints@oxfordshire.gov.uk.
  • Owners may face tax bills or prosecution when selling property.
  • If no action, EA, OCC and owners should be named.
